Sir. Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya

1861-1962

Father of Engineering

Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya was born on 15 September, 1861, near Chikkaballapur in South Karnataka. He went on to become India’s most prolific civil engineer, dam builder, economist, statesman, and can be counted among the last century’s foremost nation-builders. The Engineering Community across India celebrates Engineers Day on 15 September every year as a tribute to the greatest Indian Engineer Bharat Ratna Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya. "Role of Engineers in a devolping India" is the theme of Engineers Day.

Contribution

  • Sir M V was the architect of the Great Krishna Raja Sagara Dam in Mysuru.
  • He was instrumental in designing and patenting a system of automatic weir floodgates in 1903
  • He designed a flood protection system for the city of Hyderabad by Mussi River.
  • He is the one who designed a plan for road construction between Tirumala & Tirupati
  • He has served as the Diwan of the State of Mysore.
  • He was an Engineer who had planned the Indian Economy in 1934.
